Rules and information

Rules

To make the competition fair we have added some rules:

  • You need a user account on Strava to participate www.strava.com
  • You need to join your local club on Strava to participate
  • If it is not on Strava, it did not happen
  • Cheating is not allowed
  • Participating is voluntary, but by joining the club you accept that your results are a part of the competition and will be shared with Omega to be presented as a part of the accumulated total
  • You are responsible for your personal settings on the app and what you share with others.

Each week the accumulated kilometres for each club will be added up and then divided by the size of the office/location headcount.
The club with the highest median will be declared a winner by the competition committee.

Running is a high impact & cardio activity and therefore induces more stress than cycling and fairness we will be using a ratio for cycling due to the amount of kilometres that can be easily covered compared to running and other activities; for each 3km cycled = 1km run.

Once the winning club has been disclosed the committee will do a random draw between the club participants to select the lucky winner of the weekly prize.

Weekly prize: Suunto 9 Barometer sports watch with Nordic inside!

Information about the leaderboard

The leaderboard uses Strava's own API to collect the participating clubs activity.
No personal data is stored or processed.
The weeks end each sunday at 23:59 GMT+1.
